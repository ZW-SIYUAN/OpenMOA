About Us
========

OpenMOA is a research project from the `Computational Data Science and
Privacy (CDSP) Lab <https://cdsp.cs.wm.edu/>`__ at William & Mary, in
collaboration with the Adaptive Machine Learning group at Victoria University
of Wellington.

For inquiries, contact us via `Email`_ or join the `Discord`_.

Team
----

.. card:: Yi He — Project Leader (PI)

    ..  grid:: 2

        ..  grid-item::
            :columns: 12

            Yi He is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science
            at William & Mary and the Principal Investigator of the CDSP Lab.
            His research spans data mining, streaming algorithms, graph learning,
            and AI for conservation. He received his Ph.D. from the University of
            Louisiana at Lafayette in 2020 (advised by Xindong Wu) and his B.E.
            from Harbin Institute of Technology in 2013. He previously held a
            position at Old Dominion University. He is a recipient of the
            IEEE TCII Volunteer Award (2022) and the NSF CRII Award (2023).

.. card:: Zhili Wang — Lead Developer

    ..  grid:: 2

        ..  grid-item::
            :columns: 12

            Zhili Wang is a Ph.D. student at William & Mary in the CDSP Lab and
            the lead developer of OpenMOA. His work focuses on online learning
            under dynamic feature spaces, with an emphasis on algorithm design,
            implementation, and reproducible benchmarking.

            * :fab:`github` :github:`ZW-SIYUAN`

            Heitor Murilo Gomes is a Senior Lecturer at Victoria University of
            Wellington (VuW) in New Zealand. Previously, he served as
            Co-Director of the AI Institute at the University of Waikato (UoW).
            His research focuses on machine learning for data streams, covering
            ensemble learning, semi-supervised learning, drift detection, and
            online continual learning. He has contributed to several open-source
            stream learning projects, most notably MOA (Massive Online Analysis),
            for over a decade.
